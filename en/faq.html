Frequently Asked Questions

All the answers to your questions before, during and after your consultation.

Before the consultation

Do I need a referral to book an appointment?
No. Booking is direct and open via our online reservation platform. No prior medical prescription is required.
Do I need to come on an empty stomach?
No. The procedure uses only local anaesthesia. Fasting is not necessary.
Can I bring my children?
Yes. Any examination of a minor is performed in the presence of a parent or legal guardian, with their explicit prior consent.
What should I bring?
At minimum, bring your identity card. If a medical indication is established during the consultation, your health insurance card will be needed to benefit from partial reimbursement.

During the consultation

How does the consultation work?
Your surgeon examines your lesions in a targeted manner using the Heine DeltaOne dermatoscope. They provide a detailed diagnosis and discuss surgical treatment options with you if needed. The consultation lasts 20 to 30 minutes for an assessment alone.
Can the removal be done on the same day?
In the vast majority of cases, yes. We recommend mentioning this when booking so that the time slot can be arranged accordingly. Our clinic is equipped to perform the excision on the same day.
Is the anaesthesia painful?
The local injection causes a brief, mild sensation of pressure or stinging. Once the anaesthetic takes effect — within a few seconds — you will feel no pain from the procedure.

After the removal

When are the stitches removed?
This protocol is defined by your surgeon. As a general rule: 8 days for lesions on the face, 10 to 15 days for those on the trunk or limbs.
Can I resume normal activities?
Daily and professional activities can generally be resumed immediately. It is advisable to avoid intense physical exertion (sport, heavy lifting) for 48 to 72 hours depending on the location of the removal.
When will I receive the histopathological results?
Generally within 10 to 15 business days. Our secretary or your surgeon will contact you as soon as the findings are received from the certified laboratory.
Will there be a visible scar?
Any skin incision leaves a scar. However, the expertise of a plastic surgeon ensures it is as discreet as possible: optimal placement along skin tension lines, ultra-fine sutures, scar repositioned within natural folds. It tends to whiten and fade significantly over time.

Pricing & Reimbursement

Is my removal reimbursed?
If the removal is performed for a medical indication (suspicious or atypical naevus confirmed by the surgeon), it is partially reimbursed by your Belgian health insurer. If the removal is purely cosmetic, it is not reimbursed by the basic health insurance.
How do I claim the reimbursement?
Your surgeon is an accredited healthcare provider. At the end of the consultation, they will issue a proof-of-care certificate that you can submit directly to your health insurer — no additional steps required.
